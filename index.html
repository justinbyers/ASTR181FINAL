The Sombrero Galaxy

Description: A type Sa-Sb spiral galaxy also known as Messier 104.

The Sombrero Galaxy is named such due to the similarities of its bright core and dark dust ring and the shape of a sombrero hat.


- Apparent Mag: 8.0

- Size: ~50,000 light years across

- Distance Away: ~28 million light years

- Notable features include a bright core with a prominent dust lane ring surrounding the spiral arms. M104 is believed to host a massive black hole in the center, as suggested by X-ray emissions.

The Crab Nebula

Description: A widely known supernova remnant also known as Messier 1.

The Crab Nebula is a relatively young object, aged at around 1,000 years old.


- Apparent Mag: 8.4

- Size: ~11 light years across

- Distance Away: 63,000 light years


- Notable features include a young rapidly spinning neutron star at its core. Messier 1 was so popular among astronomers that it divided the community in two; one half whose work related to Messier 1, and one half whose work didn't.

NGC 1365

Description: NGC 1365 is a barred spiral galaxy in the constellation Fornax.


- Apparent Mag: 9.5

- Size: ~200,000 light years across

- Distance Away: ~60 million light years


- Notable features include clear spiral arms with a barred center. NGC 1365 is known for being one of the largest known galaxies.

M66

Description: A deformed Sb spiral galaxy also known as NGC 3627.

M66 is a member of the Leo Triplet, along with M65 and NGC 3628.


- Apparent Mag: 8.9

- Size: ~100,000 light years across

- Distance Away: ~35 million light years


- Notable features include deformed and asymmetrical arms possibly due to galactic neighbor encounters

M96

Description: A type Sa spiral galaxy, also known as NGC 3368, located inside of the Leo constellation.


- Apparent Mag: 9.2

- Size: ~66,000 light years across

- Distance Away: ~38 million light years


- Notable features include faint extensions of arms that increase the galaxy's size to about 100,000 light years.

- In addition, M96 was one of the first spiral galaxies ever discovered.
